# Event Report: “AWS: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application featuring Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud”

### Purpose of the Event

- Provide a realistic overview of the Cloud Computing market.
- Share career development orientations and how to seize job opportunities.
- Guide on effectively applying AI in studying and working.
- Equip technology students with essential core skills upon graduation.

### List of Speakers

- **Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung** - Head of Solution Architecture at AWS Vietnam
- **Mr. Banh Cam Vinh** - Data Engineer at Renova Cloud
- **Ms. Nhu Tran** - Account Manager at AWS Vietnam
- **Mr. Khang Nguyen** - Solution Architect at Cloud Kinetics, Swinburne Alumni

### Key Highlights

#### The fierce labor market landscape and AI trends

- **High recruitment standards:** Businesses no longer do "hand-holding" and require even interns to know advanced concepts (like Kubernetes). An excellent degree is no longer a guarantee of employment.
- **Personnel shifts due to AI:** Decreased demand for Junior roles, increased recruitment for Seniors combined with AI Agents. However, the total number of jobs in the industry will increase due to the booming demand for technological adoption in all sectors.
- **Competitive strategy:** Position yourself in a specific domain (e.g., finance, retail) rather than just a role (e.g., Data Engineer or AI Engineer) to make your profile stand out. Break the "closed-door training" mindset and actively participate in communities to increase personal visibility.
- **AWS investments:** The cloud computing industry is booming, and AWS is investing heavily in Vietnam through infrastructure (Local Zones) and specifically local human resources training.

#### The gap between academia and corporate reality

- **Data reality is not "rosy":** At school, students often work with clean data, clear requirements, relaxed timelines, and the worst consequence is failing a subject. However, at work, data comes from complex sources, client requirements change constantly, and system crashes directly impact organizational revenue.
- **Focus on the "framework" rather than tools:** Tools change constantly, so learn and master the "DNA" or core architecture (framework) of the system. Once you understand the foundation, you can easily integrate new tools into that framework.
- **Limitations of AI:** AI can assist in writing code or generating dashboards, but it cannot replace humans in communication skills, business understanding, and architectural decision-making.

#### Overcoming fears and secrets to grasping opportunities

- **Overcoming the fear of failure and communication:** Students often carry a fear of being wrong from school and a fear of being judged when communicating with superiors. The advice is to proactively make small talk and initiate greetings. This is the simplest way to overcome shyness and make your presence known to superiors.
- **Finding the "Blue Ocean":** Good job opportunities are often not found on mass recruitment sites (considered red oceans with too much competition). Opportunities come through networks and doing things few are willing to do. Ms. Nhu joined AWS after 10 rejections thanks to her persistence and being directly introduced to the Hiring Manager by a senior manager.

#### AI-era learning mindset and early career orientation

- **Using AI properly:** You can ask AI to "outsource your thinking," but you cannot ask it to "outsource your understanding." If you use AI for assignments without understanding the root foundation, AI will only amplify your errors in practical work.
- **Criteria for choosing your first job:** When fresh out of school, salary should not be the top criterion. Consider a job based on experience, network, knowledge, and personal growth opportunities.
- **Attitude is more important than aptitude:** When evaluating a fresher, businesses rank "Attitude" as the top factor because it shows the potential for learning and long-term commitment, followed by aptitude and experience. Therefore, maintain a hunger for learning, persistence, willingness to accept mistakes, and constantly ask "Why?".

### What I Learned

#### Career Development Mindset

- **Business-first approach:** Always understand that technology exists to solve business problems. Understanding the business domain is just as important as coding skills.
- **Self-positioning:** Build a personal brand within the tech community; one cannot maintain an "introverted" mindset forever.
- **Job search strategy:** Leverage networks and target niche markets instead of competing head-on in overly crowded places.

#### Skills and Expertise

- **Learning by "framework":** Shift from memorizing how to use tools to understanding the operational principles of system architectures.
- **Using AI as an assistant:** Use AI to speed up work, but strictly maintain your own understanding of the code.

#### Attitude and Psychology

- **Fresher's Mindset:** A progressive attitude, diligence, and not being afraid of making mistakes are the greatest assets of a newly graduated student.
- **Proactive communication:** Small talk skills and initiating conversations are powerful tools to shorten the distance with managers and colleagues.

### Application to Work

- **Change the way AI is used:** Apply AI to generate code or solve bugs, but strictly require myself to explain the underlying principles of that solution.
- **Proactively expand network:** Start participating in Cloud/IT communities and practice small talk with seniors to build relationships.
- **Deepen architectural knowledge:** Spend time studying foundational elements (networking, OS, K8s) rather than just learning superficial frameworks.
- **Reorient goals:** When looking for upcoming internship/job opportunities, I will prioritize environments that allow me to learn and develop a business mindset over just looking at the allowance.

### Event Experience

Attending the **“AWS: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application featuring Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud”** workshop was an eye-opening experience, helping me face the fierce reality of the labor market and reshape my personal development roadmap. Some notable experiences:

#### Listening to insights from corporate reality

- Speakers from AWS, Renova Cloud, and Cloud Kinetics provided authentic perspectives, "shattering" illusions about clean data or easy problems on campus.
- I understood the market shift with the advent of AI, thereby knowing how to prepare my skills accordingly.

#### Overcoming personal psychological barriers

- Through the speakers' stories, I realized that the fear of failure and passivity are my biggest barriers. The advice on proactive "small talk" is truly a small skill that brings a massive impact.

#### Learning to master AI instead of depending on it

- The most valuable experience was changing my mindset on using AI: moving from viewing AI as a "lifesaver" that does my homework to seeing it as a supporting tool, while I myself must master the core "understanding."

#### Networking and exchanging

- The event created an opportunity to interact with experts and alumni, helping me realize the importance of building my own "Blue Ocean" through networking.

#### Key Takeaways

- The market is extremely fierce, but opportunities are always there for those who master the fundamentals, have a good attitude, and know how to integrate with AI.
- Do not choose the first job solely for money; choose a place that provides plenty of "stage room" and the most learning opportunities.
